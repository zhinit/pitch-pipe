# Equal Temperament

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equal_temperament
Fetched: 2026-06-14

## Definition

Equal temperament is a musical temperament or tuning system that approximates
just intervals by dividing an octave into steps such that the ratio of
frequencies of any adjacent pair of notes is the same.

## 12-Tone Equal Temperament (12-TET)

The standard Western tuning system. Divides the octave into 12 equal parts on
a logarithmic scale.

### Semitone Ratio

The ratio between adjacent semitones:

    2^(1/12) ≈ 1.059463

Each semitone represents 100 cents (1/12 of 1200 cents per octave).

### Frequency Formula

    Pn = Pa × (2^(1/12))^(n−a)

Where:
- Pn = desired pitch frequency
- Pa = reference frequency (typically A4 = 440 Hz)
- n = semitone position of desired note
- a = semitone position of reference note

### Standard Pitch Reference

"In modern times, 12 TET is usually tuned relative to a standard pitch of
440 Hz, called A440."

## Historical Adoption

"12-tone equal temperament won out," enabling "enharmonic modulation, new
styles of symmetrical tonality and polytonality, atonal music, and jazz to
develop and flourish."
