# A440 (Pitch Standard)

Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A440_(pitch_standard)
Fetched: 2026-06-14

## Definition

A440 (also called Stuttgart pitch) is the musical pitch corresponding to an
audio frequency of 440 Hz, assigned to the note A above middle C (A4 in
scientific pitch notation). It is the general tuning standard for musical
pitch, formalized as ISO 16.

## ISO 16

- 1955: Adopted by the International Organization for Standardization as
  Recommendation R 16
- 1975: Reaffirmed as ISO 16

## Historical Development

- 1834: Johann Heinrich Scheibler proposed A440 at the Society of German
  Natural Scientists and Physicians in Stuttgart, after inventing the
  "tonometer" for pitch measurement
- 1926: American music industry informally adopted 440 Hz
- 1936: American Standards Association formally recommended A440
- 1939: International conference at Broadcasting House, London (representatives
  from France, Germany, the Netherlands, Italy, and England) officially adopted
  A440. Sir James Swinburne noted "440 could be more easily factored and
  electronically synthesized" compared to the prime number 439

## Current Usage

A440 is widely used as concert pitch in the United Kingdom and the United
States. It is commonly used as a reference frequency to calibrate acoustic
equipment and to tune pianos, violins, and other musical instruments.

## Variations in Practice

- Continental European orchestras: typically 440–444 Hz
- Modern baroque pitch: 415 Hz (one semitone below A440)
- Chorton pitch (German church music): 466 Hz
- Classical pitch: 427–430 Hz

## Broadcast Reference

The US time and frequency station WWV broadcasts a 440 Hz signal at two
minutes past each hour (WWVH at the first minute), a service added in 1936.
