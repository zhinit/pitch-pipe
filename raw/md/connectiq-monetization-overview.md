## Merchant Onboarding

To sell apps, developers must:
1. Register as a merchant through Garmin's onboarding process
2. Pay an annual, non-refundable program fee of $100 USD
3. Be verified as a trader for the app to appear in the store

## Revenue Split

- Garmin takes 15% of the tax-exclusive price point
- Garmin adds sales tax onto the price point (buyer pays it)
- Garmin covers credit card processing fees
- Digital service taxes (if applicable) and currency conversion costs
  are withheld from developer payouts

## Payment Method

Users can pay with any credit card, not limited to Garmin Pay. The
system accepts payments similar to garmin.com transactions. Users
without compatible banks can use workarounds like the Curve card service.

## Geographic Availability

US, Germany, United Kingdom, Mexico, Canada, European Union countries,
Australia, and New Zealand.

## Device Requirement

Paid apps only work on CIQ System 7+ devices (API Level 5.0+). Older
devices cannot purchase or unlock paid apps through Garmin's system.

## Refund Policy

48-hour no-questions-asked refund guarantee for buyers.

## Promo Codes

Developers can issue promotional codes. Garmin provides promo codes to
help migrate users who previously purchased through external payment
systems (like PayPal).

## App Ownership

Purchases are on a user account basis, not per-device. Users can install
on multiple compatible devices.

## Pricing

Garmin controls pricing within tiers. Minimum observed price is $4.99.
Garmin automatically adjusts prices across countries.

## Developer Experience

From forum discussion:
- The $100 annual fee is seen as a filter for casual developers
- Comparable to Apple's $100/year developer program
- One developer noted it's more expensive than their prior PayPal setup
  ($23.99/year + 7.69% per transaction)
- No respondents reported concrete sales increases after switching to
  Garmin's system
- Garmin stopped providing actual download counts — developers only see
  vague ranges like "1000+"
- Some older devices (e.g. FR945) don't support Garmin's monetization
  system due to firmware requirements

## Previous System

Before Garmin's official monetization (launched August 2024):
- Developers used workarounds like PayPal donation links
- Garmin stated they didn't "review or manage these app payment services"
- Some developers still use external payment systems
